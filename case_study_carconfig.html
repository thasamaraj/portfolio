AI-Assisted Car Configurator

Redefining how users explore, personalize, and experience car ownership…

Role Senior UX Designer
Scope Research, flows, concept, prototype
Time 8–10 weeks
Constraints No prod build · tight team
Context & Goal

What we aimed to prove

Validate a simpler, AI-assisted configuration that reduces decision fatigue and creates an emotional “see-my-car-in-my-world” moment.

Success proxy: positive usability signals on flow clarity and “try-on/backdrop” desirability; stakeholder alignment for pilot.
Research (Focused)

How we learned fast

  • Reviewed leading configurators (BMW, Audi, Totem) to spot gaps in lifestyle visualization.
  • Lightweight user conversations with car shoppers & showroom staff → friction themes.
  • Artifact audit of current assets & tech feasibility with team.
Key Pain Points

What gets in the way

  • Option overload: too many trims/specs to compare.
  • Low imagination support: hard to picture the car in personal context.
  • Channel break: showroom → mobile handoff is weak.
User Flow

Shortest path to confidence

  1. Prompt (e.g., “SUV for weekend family trips”).
  2. AI proposes model + reasons (explainable panel).
  3. Quick edit: exterior / interior essentials.
  4. Experience: Try-On or Backdrop Magic.
  5. Save → QR → continue on mobile.

Concept Sketches

We sketched to align fast

Design Decisions

What we chose (and why)

AI first entry

Prompt trims choices → reduces initial cognitive load.

Explainable recommendations

“Why this model & package” increases trust.

Experience as proof

Try-On / Backdrop Magic to create emotional fit quickly.

Handoff

QR → mobile to keep momentum post-showroom.

Outcomes & Next

What moved & what’s next

  • Stakeholders aligned on AI-assisted flow and experience layer value.
  • Prototype validated core desirability in quick tests.
  • Next: expand test set, add analytics, refine explainability & accessibility, pilot in one showroom.
